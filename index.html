The dawn of books
When Venice made the world read

A little tribute

This is a self-learning space inspired by the book Los primeros editores by Alessandro Marzo Magno (Malpaso, 2018). Yes, Spanish edition. Google Translate does the rest.

The idea is to present the book to motivate its reading. So I have taken the review and some lines from the book -appropriately quoted-, the cover image of the edition and the layout to recreate this website. The rest is its own content.

Reading in italics

This is the story of how Venice was a multinational of books, with the largest typographic industries of the time, which allowed printing, in almost any language, halt of the books published throughout Europe.

Aldo Manuzio is the genius who invents the figure of the modern editor. He embarks on the project of publishing Greco-Latin classics in Italian in order to print the books with the greatest circulation and invents a new typeface, italics.
